SECTION 420: Cheating and Dishonestly Inducing Delivery of Property
============================================================

Definitions:
  "cheating" means Deceiving a person fraudulently or dishonestly
  "inducing" means Causing a person to act based on deception
  "delivery" means Handing over property to another
  "valuable_security" means Document purporting to create, transfer, or release any right

Elements of the offence:
  Physical element (actus reus): Cheating under Section 415
  Physical element (actus reus): Dishonestly inducing delivery of property
  Circumstance: Delivery of property or valuable security
  Circumstance: Making, altering, or destroying valuable security

Penalty:
  Shall be punished with imprisonment for a term of not less than 1 year and not more than 10 years, or with a fine of not less than S$0.00 and not more than S$50,000.00, or with any combination thereof.

Illustrations:

example1 A deceives B into believing A is a licensed contractor. B pays A in advance for work A never intends to do. A is guilty under Section 420.
example2 A, by false pretenses, induces B to sign a document transferring property to A. A is guilty under Section 420.
