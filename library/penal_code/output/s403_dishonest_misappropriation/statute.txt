SECTION 403: Dishonest Misappropriation of Property
============================================================

Definitions:
  "misappropriation" means Dishonestly converting to own use property in possession
  "movable_property" means Corporeal property except land and things attached to earth
  "dishonestly" means With intention of causing wrongful gain or wrongful loss
  "conversion" means Dealing with property inconsistent with the rights of the true owner

Elements of the offence:
  Circumstance: Property comes into possession of the accused
  Mental element (mens rea): Dishonest intention
  Physical element (actus reus): Converting property to own use
  Physical element (actus reus): Disposing of property contrary to owner's rights

Penalty:
  Shall be punished with imprisonment for a term of not less than no time and not more than 2 years, or with a fine of not less than S$0.00 and not more than S$10,000.00, or with any combination thereof.

Illustrations:

example1 A finds a government promissory note. Not knowing who lost it and believing it can be kept, A appropriates it. A may not be guilty as there was no dishonest intent.
example2 A finds a ring on the highway near B's house. A knows the ring likely belongs to B. A sells the ring without attempting to find the owner. A is guilty of misappropriation.
