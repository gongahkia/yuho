SECTION 503: Criminal Breach of Trust
============================================================

Definitions:
  "breach_of_trust" means Dishonestly misappropriating property entrusted to the accused
  "entrustment" means Being given property or dominion over property
  "misappropriation" means Using property in a manner inconsistent with the trust
  "dominion" means Control or authority over property

Elements of the offence:
  Circumstance: Being entrusted with property or dominion over property
  Mental element (mens rea): Dishonest intention
  Physical element (actus reus): Misappropriating the property
  Physical element (actus reus): Converting property to own use
  Physical element (actus reus): Using or disposing in violation of law
  Physical element (actus reus): Using or disposing in violation of contract

Penalty:
  Shall be punished with imprisonment for a term of not less than no time and not more than 7 years, or with a fine of not less than S$0.00 and not more than S$15,000.00, or with any combination thereof.

Illustrations:

example1 A is entrusted with B's property. A dishonestly sells the property and keeps the money. A commits criminal breach of trust.
example2 A, a warehouse keeper, is entrusted with goods. A dishonestly pledges the goods for a personal loan. A commits criminal breach of trust.
example3 A, an employee, is given money to purchase supplies. A spends the money on personal expenses. A commits criminal breach of trust.
