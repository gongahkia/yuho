SECTION 499: Defamation
============================================================

Definitions:
  "defamation" means Making imputation concerning person intending to harm reputation
  "imputation" means A statement suggesting something discreditable about a person
  "reputation" means The estimation in which a person is held by others
  "publication" means Communication of the imputation to a third party

Elements of the offence:
  Physical element (actus reus): Making or publishing an imputation
  Physical element (actus reus): By words spoken, written, signs, or visible representations
  Mental element (mens rea): Intending to harm reputation
  Mental element (mens rea): Knowing imputation will harm reputation
  Circumstance: Harm to reputation of the person

Penalty:
  Shall be punished with imprisonment for a term of not less than no time and not more than 2 years, or with a fine of not less than S$0.00 and not more than S$5,000.00, or with any combination thereof.

Exceptions:
  [truthPublicGood] If It is not defamation to impute anything which is true, if it is for the public good that the imputation should be made
    Then: Not defamation
  [publicConduct] If It is not defamation to express in good faith any opinion respecting the conduct of a public servant in the discharge of his public functions
    Then: Not defamation
  [courtProceedings] If It is not defamation to publish a substantially true report of the proceedings of a court of justice
    Then: Not defamation
  [goodFaithOpinion] If It is not defamation to express in good faith any opinion respecting the merits of any case decided by a court of justice
    Then: Not defamation
  [goodFaithAccusation] If It is not defamation to make in good faith an accusation against any person to any of those who have lawful authority over that person
    Then: Not defamation
  [cautionGoodFaith] If It is not defamation to convey a caution in good faith to one person against another, for the good of the person to whom the caution is conveyed
    Then: Not defamation

Illustrations:

example1 A says 'B is a thief' intending to harm B's reputation. A defames B.
example2 A publishes true facts about B's public conduct as a public servant for public good. A does not defame B.
example3 A makes good faith accusations to proper authority about B. A does not defame B.
